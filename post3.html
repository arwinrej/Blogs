What I Learned from an Unplugged Weekend

I spent one weekend offline: no social media, no endless scrolling, and no background noise. At first, it felt uncomfortable. Then it felt clear.

Without constant notifications, I noticed how often I reached for distraction. Replacing that impulse with reading, cooking, and walking made the day feel longer and more intentional.

The goal isn't to reject technology. It's to use it with awareness—and leave enough room for real attention.
